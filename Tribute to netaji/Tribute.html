Subhas Chandra Bose

Subhas Chandra Bose (born c. January 23, 1897, Cuttack, Orissa [now Odisha], India—died August 18, 1945, Taipei, Taiwan?) was an Indian revolutionary prominent in the independence movement against British rule of India. He also led an Indian national force from abroad against the Western powers during World War II. He was a contemporary of Mohandas K. Gandhi, at times an ally and at other times an adversary. Bose was known in particular for his militant approach to independence and for his push for socialist policies.

Biography Of Netaji

  • Early Life

    on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha, India, to Prabhavati Dutt Bose and Janakinath Bose Father was a successful lawyer and received the title of “Rai Bahadur” Educated at the Protestant European School (now Stewart High School) in Cuttack, alongside his siblings

  • Career and Politics

    Passed the civil service examination but resigned his candidacy and returned to India after hearing of nationalist turmoils Reflected a more militant and socialist approach to India’s independence movement, differing from Mahatma Gandhi’s less confrontational stance Formed the Forward Bloc and later the Indian National Army (INA) to fight against British rule Led the INA, comprising around 40,000 troops, against Western powers during World War II, supported by Japan

  • Leadership and Legacy

    Known for his bravery, leadership, and unwavering commitment to India’s independence Despite his unconventional approach, he remained a hero among many Indians His legacy is marked by controversy, with some criticizing his wartime alliances with Nazi Germany and Imperial Japan The Indian government has officially recognized his contributions, and various awards and films have been dedicated to his memory
